Assuming monotonicity what can be achieved? {#assuming-monotonicity-what-can-be-achieved .unnumbered}

Trying to come up with the most general form of such an elicitation. There \(N\) sets of payment options \(\mathcal{S}=\left(S_{1},…,S_{N}\right)\). From each set, the subject is asked to choose an option \(a_{i}\in S_{i}\). A set is chosen with probability \(P_{i}\) such that \(\sum_{i=1}^{N}p_{i}=1\) and \(p_{i}>0\). If set \(S_{i}\) is chosen subject is compensated by \(a_{i}\).

### Mapping Common Techniques into This Language {#mapping-common-techniques-into-this-language .unnumbered}

BDM. The bundle \(\left(1,m\right)\) is “receives item and has final wealth \(m\).” The bundle \(\left(0,m\right)\) is “does not receive item and has final wealth \(m\).” Let the subject’s initial wealth be \(W\). Let the max price is 100.

\[\mathcal{S}=\left(\left{ \left(0,W\right),\left(1,W-0.01\right)\right} ,\left{ \left(0,W\right),\left(1,W-0.02\right)\right} ,…,\left{ \left(0,W\right),\left(1,W-100\right)\right} \right)\]

Each \(S_{i}\) is chosen with equal probability.

H&L. $ \[ \mathcal{S}=\left(\left{ \left($2\circ\frac{1}{10},$1.60\circ\frac{9}{10}\right),\left($3.85\circ\frac{1}{10},$0.10\circ\frac{9}{10}\right)\right} ,\left{ \left($2\circ\frac{2}{10},$1.60\circ\frac{8}{10}\right),\left($3.85\circ\frac{2}{10},$0.10\circ\frac{8}{10}\right)\right} ,…,\left{ \left($2\circ\frac{10}{10},$1.60\circ\frac{0}{10}\right),\left($3.85\circ\frac{10}{10},$0.10\circ\frac{0}{10}\right)\right} \right) \] $ Each \(S_{i}\) is chosen with equal probability.

Ternary Price List. Let \(E\) be the lottery “Prize A if event E”. Let \(O_{p}\) be the lottery “Prize A with probability \(p\)”.

\[\mathcal{S}=\left(\left{ E,E',O_{0.5}\right} ,\left{ E,E',O_{0.51}\right} ,…,\left{ E,E',O_{1}\right} \right)\]

Each \(S_{i}\) is chosen with equal probability.

*“Crossover” Probability Elicitation.***

\[\mathcal{S}=\left(\left{ E,O_{0}\right} ,\left{ E,E',O_{0.01}\right} ,…,\left{ E,E',O_{1}\right} \right)\]

Each \(S_{i}\) is chosen with equal probability.

Cobb-Douglass Parameter Calibration. Let \(\left(x_{1},x_{2}\right)\) be a bundle of two goods with quantities \(x_{i}\) of good \(i\). Limit the maximum amount of good 2 in the eliciation to 100.

\[\mathcal{S}=\left(\left{ \left(2,2\right),\left(1,2\right)\right} ,\left{ \left(2,2\right),\left(1,3\right)\right} ,…,\left{ \left(2,2\right),\left(1,100\right)\right} \right)\]
